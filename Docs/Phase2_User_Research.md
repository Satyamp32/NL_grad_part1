# 👥 Phase 2: Primary Research & User Validation
### Spotify Music Discovery — User Research Deliverables
**Based on survey results (N=10) · Cross-validated with 1,466 AI-analyzed reviews**

---

## 2.1 Target Segment Selection (Phase 1 → Phase 2 Bridge)

Based on Phase 1's AI Discovery Engine analysis, the primary target segment for Phase 2 research is:

> **Free-Tier Active Listeners with Passive Discovery Frustration**
> Users who regularly engage with Spotify (daily or near-daily), rely heavily on their own manually curated playlists as a *workaround* for algorithm failure, and consistently feel trapped in a repetition loop despite wanting to discover new music.

**Why this segment:**
- **70% of survey respondents** are Free-tier users → largest addressable segment.
- **5/7 Free-tier respondents** rated Mood-based assistant "Very Useful" → strongest product-market fit signal.
- This segment shows the highest algorithm distrust (manually curating playlists rather than relying on Discover Weekly/Daily Mixes).
- Their frustration is not feature-gated (Premium users have access to everything and still feel it) → it's a *systemic* discovery problem.

**Segment Profile:**
- Listens to music daily (commute, work, leisure).
- Has 3+ custom playlists they've built themselves.
- Opens Spotify with a specific mood/goal but often defaults to familiar playlists.
- Has tried Discover Weekly or Daily Mixes but didn't find them reliably useful.
- Has never explored "smart shuffle" deeply because they don't trust the output.

## 2.2 User Interview Guide
### Spotify Music Discovery — 30-Minute Semi-Structured Interview

**Interviewer Instructions:**
- This is a conversational, not interrogative session. Follow the user's energy.
- Silence is okay — let the user think. Don't rush to fill pauses.
- Every question has a "probe" — use it when the user gives a shallow answer.
- Record with permission (Otter.ai / Zoom / WhatsApp voice note).
- The goal is to understand *behavior and emotion*, not to validate our hypotheses.

---

### 📍 Block 1: Intro & Warm-Up (5 minutes)

**Opening script:**
> "Thanks for taking the time. This is completely informal — there are no right or wrong answers. I'm trying to understand how real Spotify users experience music, not test the app. We'll just have a conversation. Okay if I record this so I can take better notes later?"

**Q1.1 — Music in daily life**
> "Tell me about your relationship with music. When do you typically listen? How does it fit into your day?"

*Probe if needed:* "Is it mostly background listening, or do you actively sit down and engage with it?"

**Q1.2 — Spotify history**
> "How long have you been using Spotify? What made you stick with it over other platforms?"

*Probe if needed:* "Have you ever switched or seriously considered switching to something else?"

---

### 📍 Block 2: Current Habits & Discovery Rituals (10 minutes)

**Q2.1 — Last discovery moment**
> "Walk me through the last time you actively wanted to find something *new* to listen to. What triggered it? What did you do next?"

*Probe if needed:* "Did you use Spotify for that? Why or why not?"

**Q2.2 — Typical session start**
> "When you open Spotify right now — like today — what do you usually tap first? Why that?"

*Probe if needed:* "How often does what Spotify shows you on the home screen actually match what you're in the mood for?"

**Q2.3 — Playlist behavior**
> "Tell me about your own playlists. How many do you have? How did they get built?"

*Probe if needed:* "Do you add to them often, or do you keep the same songs for months?"

**Q2.4 — Discovery sources outside Spotify**
> "If you discover a great new song or artist — where does that usually happen? Spotify? YouTube? TikTok? A friend?"

*Probe if needed:* "Why that source and not Spotify's built-in discovery tools?"

---

### 📍 Block 3: Friction Points & Repetition (10 minutes)

**Q3.1 — The repetition experience**
> "How often do you feel like Spotify is playing the same songs over and over? Walk me through what that feels like."

*Probe if needed:* "When you notice it happening, what do you do? Skip? Change playlist? Give up?"

**Q3.2 — The escape attempt**
> "Have you ever tried to *escape* that repetition loop? Like, tried something in Spotify that was supposed to give you something new? What happened?"

*Probe if needed:* "Did Discover Weekly, Smart Shuffle, or the AI DJ help at all? What worked, what didn't?"

**Q3.3 — The workaround**
> "What's your go-to move when you want something different but don't know what?"

*Probe if needed:* "Does that always work? What's the most frustrating part of that process?"

**Q3.4 — Mood vs. algorithm**
> "Has Spotify ever recommended you a song that was perfectly right for the moment? Tell me about that. What made it feel right?"

*Probe if needed:* "Could you have described that feeling to Spotify in words beforehand? Like, could you have *typed* what you wanted?"

---

### 📍 Block 4: Feature Assessment & Future Vision (5 minutes)

**Q4.1 — Feature awareness**
> "Are you aware of features like Smart Shuffle, Discover Weekly, or the AI DJ? Have you tried them?"

*Probe if needed:* "What did you think? Does it feel like it actually knows you, or is it guessing?"

**Q4.2 — Ideal future state**
> "If Spotify were perfect — like, it understood exactly what you wanted to hear at any moment — what would that look like? How would it work?"

*Probe if needed:* "Would you want to *tell* it your mood? Or should it figure it out? Should it explain its choices?"

**Q4.3 — Closing**
> "Is there anything about your Spotify experience — frustrating, surprising, delightful — that you think I should know about? Something you've always wanted to tell the app?"

---

### Closing Script
> "This has been incredibly helpful. Would you be open to a 5-minute follow-up if I have specific questions later? And is there anyone else in your circle who listens to a lot of music — even someone who's stopped using Spotify — that you think would have interesting things to say?"

## 2.4 Cross-Respondent Insight Synthesis

### Theme 1: The Manual Curation Workaround is Universal
**7 of 10 respondents** primarily use self-built playlists as their primary listening mode. This is not a preference — it is a *workaround* for algorithm distrust. The algorithm has trained users to give up on automated discovery.

### Theme 2: Repetition is Normalized, but Fatiguing
**8 of 9 respondents who answered** rated repetition at a **3/5 or higher**. This shows that repetition fatigue is normalized but chronic across the entire cohort. R9's extreme rating (4/5) and "Rarely" discovering tracks indicates that for a segment of users, discovery is completely blocked.

### Theme 3: Two Distinct Discovery Failure Modes
The survey revealed a clean split between two failure modes:
1.  **Echo Chamber (Trapped/Bored):** Experienced by R2, R4, R6, R9 (repetition and mainstream bias).
2.  **Discovery Paralysis (Overwhelmed):** Experienced by R5, R7, R8 ( paradox of choice).

### Theme 4: Mood-Based Discovery is the Consensus MVP
**9 of 10 respondents** rated the Mood-based assistant as useful (50% Very Useful, 40% Somewhat Useful), validating this feature as the most universally desired entry point.

## 2.6 Phase 2 → Phase 3 Handoff

**What Phase 2 confirmed:**
1.  ✅ Algorithmic repetition is chronic and normalized (**89%** of responses $\ge$ 3/5).
2.  ✅ Manual curation is the dominant workaround (**70%**).
3.  ✅ Mood-based discovery is the most demanded solution (**90%** Useful).
4.  ✅ Discovery Slider has a strong demand signal (**80%** Useful).
5.  🆕 Discovery paralysis is a real failure mode (**30%** cite "too many options").

**Problem statement for Phase 3:**
> **Free-tier Active Listeners** struggle to discover new music that matches their *emotional state and listening context* when they open Spotify, because the recommendation engine surfaces familiar/mainstream content by default and the home screen is too overwhelming to navigate toward something new. This causes them to fall back on manually curated playlists as a workaround, reducing their engagement with Spotify's discovery surface and creating suppressed churn risk.
